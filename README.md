# COFFEE SHOP SALES ANALYSIS  

## Project Overview

The project entails carrying out analysis of the sales of coffee using Microsoft Excel, with the aim of creating key insights based on interactive dashboards. This is with the aim of changing the way raw sales data relates to business decision-making. The dashboard will illustrate sales trends, product sales, and customer behavior using the built-in analytical and visualization capabilities of Microsoft Excel.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Evaluate overall business performance using KPIs such as Total Revenue, Footfall, Average Bill per Person, and Average Orders per Person.
- Identify highest and lowest performing stores, products, and time periods.
- Analyze sales patterns across hours, days, and months.
- Assess customer spending behavior and store-level efficiency.
- Convert raw transactional data into actionable insights using an interactive dashboard.

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains transactional records including:

- Transaction ID  
- Store Location  
- Product Category and Product Name  
- Quantity and Unit Price  
- Transaction Date and Time  

*Footfall is calculated using transaction count due to absence of unique customer identities.*

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- Data Cleaning Techniques

## 📊 Key Insights

### 💠 Revenue per Customer Analysis

To evaluate how effectively each store converts customer traffic into revenue, Average Revenue per Customer (AOV) was calculated as:

**AOV = Total Revenue ÷ Total Transactions**

Although Hell’s Kitchen records the highest footfall and total revenue, Lower Manhattan generates the highest revenue per customer.

This indicates that customers at Lower Manhattan tend to purchase more items or higher-value products per visit. Possible contributing factors include effective upselling, better product placement, and stronger preference for premium offerings.

In contrast, Hell’s Kitchen relies primarily on high customer volume, with relatively lower average spending per customer.

This comparison highlights two different performance models:
- **Volume-driven growth** (Hell’s Kitchen)
- **Efficiency-driven growth** (Lower Manhattan)

From a business perspective, improving upselling strategies and product mix in high-traffic stores could significantly increase overall profitability.


### 💠 Time-Based Performance

#### 1️⃣ By Day:
- **Highest Sales Day:** Monday  
- **Lowest Sales Day:** Sunday  

The business shows strong weekday dependence.

#### 2️⃣ By Month:
- **Highest Revenue Month:** June  
- **Lowest Revenue Month:** February  

Sales increase steadily toward mid-year, indicating seasonal growth.

#### 3️⃣ By Hour:
- **Peak Hours:** 8 AM – 10 AM  
- **Lowest Activity Hours:** Late evening  

Morning demand significantly drives total revenue.  

---
### 💠 Product Performance

- **Highest Revenue Category:** Coffee ($269,952.45)  
- **Lowest Revenue Category:** Packaged Chocolate ($4,407.64)  

Coffee generates the highest sales and is the main contributor to total revenue. This shows that most customers prefer coffee and purchase it regularly.

Packaged Chocolate has the lowest sales among all categories, indicating low customer demand and limited contribution to overall revenue.

The business is highly dependent on coffee for income. Improving the promotion and visibility of low-performing categories like Packaged Chocolate can help balance revenue and reduce dependency on a single product group.



---

### 💠 Customer Behavior

Revenue and footfall show a strong positive relationship, meaning higher customer traffic directly increases total sales.

--- 

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Increase staffing and inventory during peak morning hours.
-  Use high-performing locations for premium product positioning.

This dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of coffee shop performance by identifying both highest and lowest performing areas across stores, time periods, and product categories. The insights support data-driven decisions in operations, inventory planning, and revenue strategy.
